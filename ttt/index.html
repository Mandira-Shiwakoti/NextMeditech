Who We Are

A youth‑led movement empowering communities to achieve Zero by 30

Welcome to the Youth Against Rabies campaign, a youth-led movement dedicated to achieving the global "Zero by 30" goal—to end human deaths from dog-mediated rabies by 2030.

This initiative is a collaborative effort by WHO Regional Office for South-East Asia, WOAH Asia and the Pacific, and Nymat, empowering passionate young people to become active participants in rabies prevention and control.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To build a global network of youth champions dedicated to raising awareness, building technical capacity, and fostering community-led action for rabies prevention. Through education, advocacy, and collaboration, we aim to drive the global "Zero by 30" goal.

Our Vision

A world free from human rabies deaths, where every community is empowered with the knowledge and tools to prevent and respond to this devastating disease.

Our Implementation Strategy

Combining youth-led local action with global expertise and resources

1. Phased, Youth-Led Model

The campaign is driven by youth champions through a clear, three-phase roadmap to create real-world change.

2. Partner-Driven Training

Training and resources from WHO, WOAH, GARC, and WVS ensure accuracy, quality, and impact.

3. Community-Focused Action

Champions lead workshops, vaccination drives, and awareness campaigns that directly serve local needs.
